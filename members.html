Latest Update - Ongoing

Flag Ninjas Activity Patterns

Our surveillance and community reports have identified patterns in Flag Ninjas operations: They typically operate between 11 PM and 3 AM, targeting newly installed flags within 24-48 hours. We've documented their methods and are tracking their movements across Kent.

Current Status: We have identified multiple locations where they have been active. Our team is monitoring these areas and documenting all incidents for our legal case.

Recent Intelligence

Donation Page Investigation

Their Buy Me a Coffee page (https://buymeacoffee.com/favninjas) continues to receive funding from 87 supporters as of 3rd November 2025. We are documenting all available information about this page and its supporters as part of our legal case.

Legal Action: Our legal team is investigating whether the donation platform can be held accountable for facilitating funding of criminal activities. We are building a comprehensive case that includes evidence of the direct link between donations and criminal acts.

Active Mission

Flag Campaign - Kent Coverage

Current Operations: We continue our flag campaigns across Kent, replacing stolen flags and maintaining our displays. Despite ongoing theft, we remain committed to honoring our fallen heroes.

Maintenance Protocol: Our team conducts regular maintenance on all flag displays, ensuring they remain in the most presentable condition. We also remove cable ties left behind by The Flag Ninjas, cleaning up after their vandalism.

Safety Measures: Due to reports of armed individuals, we have implemented safety protocols for all flag installation operations. Team members are briefed on security measures and we maintain communication throughout operations.

Legal Mission

Building Our Case

Legal Proceedings: We are working with legal professionals to build a comprehensive case against The Flag Ninjas and their supporters. With over ¬£1,000 in stolen property, this represents a substantial amount of theft under UK law.

Key Legal Points:

  • Theft Act 1968 - Theft of property over ¬£1,000
  • Criminal Damage Act 1971 - Vandalism and property damage
  • Proceeds of Crime Act 2002 - Confiscation of assets from criminal conduct
  • Aggravating factors: Systematic, repeated theft with substantial value

We are committed to ensuring those responsible face the full force of the law.
